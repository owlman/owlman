## What I Do on GitHub?

As a technical writer and computer systems learner focused on long-term accumulation and reusable technical judgment, I mainly dedicate myself to organizing computer fundamentals, toolchain tradeoffs, writing methods, and experimental records, encompassing the following areas:

1. **Long-Term Computer Science Notes**
   - Algorithms, operating systems, compiler principles, and related foundational pathways
   - Learning insights on Python, JavaScript, C++, and other languages

2. **Development Tools and Engineering Practice**
   - Front-end and back-end frameworks: Node.js, Vue.js, uni-app practice summaries
   - Editors and IDEs: NVim, VS Code, OpenCode experience
   - Operations and deployment: Git, Docker, Kubernetes practical records

3. **Technical Writing and Reproducible Experiments**
   - Using Obsidian, Markdown, and LaTeX as writing tools
   - Recording complete experimental processes (environment, commands, results) for reproducibility
   - Polishing learning notes into articles with strong technical judgment

My goal is to ensure that the content I share on GitHub has long-term value, helps readers build systematic knowledge, and remains reproducible. To that end, I try to avoid the following:

- Publishing clickbait or emotional content
- Using AI as a substitute for thinking
- Chasing short-term trends or traffic dividends
- Pretending to know everything

### Who Should Follow Me?

- Readers who want to know the "why" rather than just the "how"
- People who need complete reasoning, trade-off processes, and failure records
- Learners who value long-term accumulation and systematic learning over quick fixes
